Privacy Policy

Recordings without a Google account

By default, phonemizer.live doesn't require an account. Audio you record and the transcripts/spectrograms derived from it are stored on the server that runs the app, keyed to an anonymous identifier kept in your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent to a third party in this mode.

Recordings with a Google account

If you choose to connect a Google account ("Connect Google Drive" in Settings), phonemizer.live requests permission to:

  • See your Google account email address, used only to show which account is connected — it is not used for marketing or shared with anyone.
  • Create and manage files in a single Drive folder named "phonemizer.live recordings" (the drive.file scope). This scope only grants access to files the app itself creates — phonemizer.live cannot see, read, or modify any other file in your Drive.

When connected, new recordings (audio and spectrogram images) are uploaded directly to that folder in your own Drive instead of being stored on the phonemizer.live server. Deleting a recording in the app deletes the corresponding files from your Drive. Disconnecting your account in Settings stops new recordings from going to Drive and reverts the app to the anonymous, server-stored mode described above — it does not delete files already in your Drive, and you're free to delete the "phonemizer.live recordings" folder yourself at any time.

You can revoke phonemizer.live's access at any time from your Google Account permissions page, independent of anything in this app.

What we don't do

phonemizer.live does not sell, rent, or share your recordings, transcripts, or account information with third parties, and does not use your data for advertising. Google user data obtained through Google sign-in is used solely to provide the Drive-sync feature described above.

phonemizer.live's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.
